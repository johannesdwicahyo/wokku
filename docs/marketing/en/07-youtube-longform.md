# YouTube Long-Form Strategy

> Secondary channel, not your primary. 1-2 videos per month. Higher production, longer videos (8-20 min). Purpose: credibility, SEO, attracting higher-intent viewers.

---

## Why YouTube Long-Form Matters (Later, Not Now)

**Don't start YouTube in your first month.** Start short-form content first, validate your voice and topics, THEN add YouTube when you have 500+ followers elsewhere.

**Why wait:**
- YouTube has the highest bar for production quality
- One YouTube video takes 5-15 hours to make
- Algorithm rewards subscribers, which you don't have yet
- Without cross-promotion from other platforms, first videos get zero views
- Requires clearer audio, better editing, thumbnails

**When to start YouTube:**
- After 30 days of short-form content
- When you have 500+ followers across TikTok/IG combined
- When you've identified 3-5 topics that resonate with your audience
- When you have 2+ hours of focused time per video

## Video Types for YouTube

### Type 1: Deep Dive Tutorials (15-20 min)
Long-form walkthroughs of building a feature with AI.

**Example title:** "Building Wokku's PR Preview Feature in Real-Time with Claude Code"

**Structure:**
- 0-30s: Hook + what we're building
- 30s-2min: Context (why this feature matters)
- 2-4min: Planning the approach
- 4-14min: Live coding with Claude Code
- 14-16min: Testing and review
- 16-18min: Deploy and verify
- 18-20min: Retrospective (what worked, what didn't)

**Why it works:**
- Long-tail SEO (people search for "how to build X")
- High watch time (algorithm loves this)
- Deep trust building (viewers see full process)

### Type 2: Tool Reviews / Comparisons (10-15 min)
Side-by-side comparisons of AI dev tools.

**Example title:** "Claude Code vs Cursor vs Copilot: 30 Days of Real Usage"

**Structure:**
- 0-30s: Hook (best tool is...)
- 30s-1min: Disclaimer + criteria
- 1-3min: Tool 1 strengths/weaknesses
- 3-5min: Tool 2 strengths/weaknesses
- 5-7min: Tool 3 strengths/weaknesses
- 7-10min: Direct comparisons (same task, 3 tools)
- 10-12min: My personal stack
- 12-15min: Recommendations by use case

**Why it works:**
- "Tool X vs Tool Y" is an evergreen search query
- People actively looking to make a decision
- Builds authority

### Type 3: Veteran Perspectives (8-12 min)
Long-form versions of your "15 years in, here's what I learned" content.

**Example title:** "What 15 Years of Coding Taught Me About AI-Assisted Development"

**Structure:**
- 0-1min: Hook + who you are
- 1-3min: Story from your career
- 3-5min: Lesson from that experience
- 5-7min: How it applies to AI-assisted dev today
- 7-10min: Practical examples
- 10-12min: Summary + what I'd tell my younger self

**Why it works:**
- Emotional resonance
- Unique angle only you have
- High shareability

### Type 4: Building in Public Recaps (10-15 min)
Monthly or quarterly deep dives on building Wokku.

**Example title:** "3 Months Building Wokku Solo: Numbers, Lessons, and What's Next"

**Structure:**
- 0-30s: Hook (3 months ago I started...)
- 30s-3min: Numbers (signups, revenue, features shipped)
- 3-7min: What worked (2-3 wins)
- 7-10min: What didn't work (honest failures)
- 10-12min: What I'd do differently
- 12-15min: Next quarter plan

**Why it works:**
- Transparency builds trust
- Indie hacker audience loves this
- Compounds as your story grows

### Type 5: Code Walkthroughs (5-10 min, shorter format)
Focused breakdowns of specific code or architecture.

**Example title:** "How Wokku Handles Deployments (Architecture Walkthrough)"

**Structure:**
- 0-30s: What we'll cover
- 30s-2min: System overview diagram
- 2-6min: Code walkthrough
- 6-8min: Design decisions
- 8-10min: Lessons learned

**Why it works:**
- Developers love technical deep dives
- Easier to produce than tutorials
- Good for establishing technical credibility

## Production Quality Standards

### Audio (non-negotiable)
- USB mic or lavalier (see doc 05)
- No echo, no hum, no background noise
- Consistent volume across the video
- Normalize to -16 LUFS (YouTube standard)

### Video
- 1080p minimum (1440p better)
- 30fps minimum (60fps for coding footage is nice but not required)
- Stable screen recording (no dropped frames)
- Zoom in on code when reading — default screen is too small for phones

### Editing
- Cut dead air aggressively (no silence > 0.5 sec)
- Add captions (YouTube auto-gen is okay, but review errors)
- Add chapter markers (YouTube feature, helps navigation)
- Music at start/end only, quiet under voice
- End screen with next video + subscribe prompt

### Thumbnails
- Text large enough to read on phone
- Your face NOT required (you're voice-only)
- Use the product (Wokku dashboard) or tool (Claude Code) as visual
- Consistent style across videos (build brand recognition)
- Contrast: bright background or bright text

**Thumbnail text templates:**
- "15 YEARS OF CODING" + icon
- "CLAUDE CODE 30-DAY REVIEW" + tool logo
- "BUILDING A PAAS SOLO" + Wokku logo
- "AI-ASSISTED DEVELOPMENT" + code visual

## YouTube SEO (Simple Version)

### Titles
**Formula:** [Keyword] + [Hook] + [Outcome]

**Good titles:**
- "Claude Code vs Cursor: 30 Days Real Usage (Honest Review)"
- "Building a Heroku Alternative with AI: Week 1 Progress"
- "5 Claude Code Prompts I Use Every Day (as a Senior Dev)"

**Bad titles:**
- "Claude Code is amazing!" (no keywords, vague)
- "Day 1" (no context, no keywords)
- "My thoughts on AI" (too broad, no hook)

### Descriptions
**First 3 lines (visible without clicking "more"):**
1. One-line summary of video
2. Who this is for
3. What they'll learn

**Rest of description:**
- Timestamps (YouTube automatically creates chapters)
- Links (Wokku, Twitter, etc.)
- Related videos
- Hashtags (3-5)

### Tags
YouTube tags matter less than titles and descriptions, but still use them:
- Specific: "claude code", "cursor editor", "ai coding"
- Broad: "software engineering", "developer tools"
- Brand: "wokku", "heroku alternative"

### End screen
- Next video to watch (picks one of your other videos)
- Subscribe prompt
- Link to wokku.dev

## Publishing Cadence

### Realistic for solo founder with Wokku + short-form + Twitter:

**Month 2:** 1 YouTube video
**Month 3:** 1 YouTube video
**Month 4:** 2 YouTube videos
**Month 5:** 2 YouTube videos
**Month 6+:** 2-3 YouTube videos per month

Do not exceed 2 per month until you validate YouTube is worth the effort. Each video is 5-15 hours of work — 3/month is a full day of production per week.

### Batch approach

Same as short-form: batch work.

**Week 1 of month:** Plan + script (2-3 hours)
**Week 2:** Record + edit video 1 (5-8 hours)
**Week 3:** Record + edit video 2 (5-8 hours)
**Week 4:** Publish both, engage with comments

If month is busy: ship 1 video, skip the other. Better to be consistent at 1/month than erratic at 3/month.

## One Honest Warning About YouTube

YouTube is the highest-effort, slowest-payoff platform on your list.

- TikTok can go viral week 1
- Twitter builds relationships fast
- LinkedIn converts to opportunities quickly
- **YouTube takes months to compound**

Don't start YouTube because you think "serious creators have YouTube." Start it when:
1. Your short-form is working
2. You have 30-60 min videos in your head that short-form can't contain
3. You're comfortable in front of the screen recording
4. You have 5+ hours free per month for production

If any of these are missing, **skip YouTube for now.** Focus on what's working.

YouTube is additive to your strategy, not core to it. Your core is: build products + build audience on short-form.

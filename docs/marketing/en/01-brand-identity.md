## Voice Guide

### Core principles

**Be the "tired freelancer discovering something exciting" voice.**

- Honest about your level (not an AI expert, just exploring)
- Confident about your experience (15 years is real credibility)
- Opinionated but open (strong takes, willing to be wrong)
- Practical, not theoretical (show, don't lecture)

### Tone rules

**DO:**
- Use "I" not "we" (you're solo, own it)
- Say "I don't know" when you don't
- Show the mistakes, not just the wins
- Use contractions (I'm, don't, won't, that's)
- Be specific ("Claude Code helped me refactor this 200-line controller" not "AI made coding easier")
- Embrace your accent (don't try to sound American)
- Admit when you're confused

**DON'T:**
- Claim to be an AI expert
- Promise results you haven't achieved
- Use corporate speak ("leverage", "synergy", "empower")
- Oversell your product
- Apologize for your English (just speak it)
- Script yourself into sounding robotic

### Signature phrases (natural, memorable)

Use these occasionally to build a recognizable voice:

- "Okay, let me show you what I mean."
- "This is going to sound weird, but..."
- "15 years in, and I still..."
- "Here's the thing that nobody tells you about..."
- "I was skeptical, but then..."
- "Real talk — this didn't work at first."
- "Let me be honest about this."
- "Coming from [old tech], this feels like..."

## Content Principles

### The "show, don't sell" rule
Never pitch Wokku directly in a video. Wokku appears as:
- The example codebase you're working on
- The project you're adding features to
- The app you're debugging
- The backend for a demo

Viewers see you doing real work on a real product. That's 100x more persuasive than "Wokku is cool, sign up."

### The "one insight per video" rule
Each short video = one takeaway the viewer can walk away with. Not 5 tips. Not a comprehensive tutorial. One thing.

Bad: "10 ways to use Claude Code"
Good: "The one prompt I use for every refactor"

### The "imperfect on purpose" rule
Don't polish videos to death. Some "um"s, some mistakes, some retakes — these are features, not bugs. They signal authenticity.

Rule of thumb: if it takes more than 30 minutes to edit a 60-second video, you're over-polishing.

### The "timely but evergreen" rule
Every video should work 6 months from now. Avoid:
- References to today's news
- Promises about specific versions ("Claude 4.6.5 is great")
- Trends that will age badly

Prefer:
- Techniques that apply regardless of version
- Principles over tools
- Stories over news

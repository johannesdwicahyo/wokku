# Content Calendar — First 90 Days

> Realistic schedule for a solo founder building in public. Sustainable, not overwhelming.

---

## Weekly Rhythm (repeatable)

### Core weekly output:
- **3 short videos** (TikTok + IG Reels + YouTube Shorts, cross-posted)
- **3-5 tweets** (Twitter/X)
- **1 LinkedIn post**
- **1 longer content piece** (blog post OR YouTube long-form, alternating)

### Weekly time budget:
- Content creation: 4-6 hours
- Engagement/replies: 1 hour/day = 7 hours/week
- **Total: ~12 hours/week**

This is sustainable alongside Wokku development. Any more and you're neglecting the product.

---

### Month 2 (Days 31-60): Experiment

By end of month 1, you have data. Double down on what works.

**Week 5 — Experiment with format**
- Try longer videos (90-120 sec) vs shorter (30-45 sec)
- Try more opinion content vs more tutorial
- See what the audience wants

**Week 6 — Depth content**
- One deep YouTube long-form (15-25 min): "Full walkthrough building a feature with Claude Code"
- Companion blog post with code examples
- Use long-form to attract higher-intent viewers

**Week 7 — Community engagement**
- Reply to everyone, even more carefully
- Start conversations, not just broadcasting
- Retweet/share others' content (builds reciprocity)
- Join 2-3 relevant Twitter spaces or Discord servers

**Week 8 — Content repurposing**
- Take best video and turn into:
  - Long-form YouTube (15 min)
  - Blog post (2000 words)
  - Twitter thread (10 tweets)
  - LinkedIn post
- One core idea, 4 different formats, 4x the reach

---

### Month 3 (Days 61-90): Scale What Works

**Week 9 — Double down**
- Post 4-5 videos this week on whichever pillar is performing best
- Start running first paid ads ($50-100 budget) for winning content
- Analyze what content drives Wokku signups specifically

**Week 10 — Collaborations**
- Reach out to 3 developer creators for potential collab
- Guest appearance on another dev podcast/stream if possible
- Cross-promote with other indie hackers

**Week 11 — Launch moment**
- If Wokku numbers are good: do a "bigger" launch post
- Write a long-form "Wokku is live and here's why" blog

**Week 12 — Retrospective and planning**
- Full 90-day recap video
- Share metrics publicly

- Thank followers (real engagement, not generic)

## Content Batching Strategy

You will fail if you try to "make one video a day."

**Batch approach:**

### Sunday: Planning (1 hour)
- Review the week's topics
- Open all scripts you're recording Monday
- Prepare screen recordings (pre-open browser tabs, editor, terminal)
- Write tweets for the week

### Monday: Recording (2 hours)
- Record 3-5 videos back-to-back
- Same setup, same energy, different scripts
- Don't edit yet — just record

### Tuesday: Editing (2 hours)
- Edit all videos from Monday's batch
- Add captions, music, transitions
- Export and save to Buffer/native platforms

### Wednesday-Friday: Posting + Engagement
- Videos auto-post or you post manually
- Reply to comments (30 min/day)
- Write spontaneous tweets as ideas come
- No new recording

### Saturday: Off
- No content work
- Maybe read comments and respond
- Rest

### Sunday: Plan next week + long-form
- Repeat cycle
- Also: write blog post or record long-form YouTube

## Topic Rotation (avoid boredom)

To avoid viewer fatigue, rotate pillars across the week:

### Week A
- Monday: Pillar 1 (Agentic engineering)
- Wednesday: Pillar 2 (Veteran perspective)
- Friday: Pillar 3 (Building in public)

### Week B
- Monday: Pillar 3 (Building in public)
- Wednesday: Pillar 1 (Agentic engineering)
- Friday: Pillar 4 (Tool reviews)

### Week C
- Monday: Pillar 1 (Agentic engineering)
- Wednesday: Pillar 4 (Tool reviews)
- Friday: Pillar 2 (Veteran perspective)

### Week D
- Monday: Pillar 2 (Veteran perspective)
- Wednesday: Pillar 1 (Agentic engineering)
- Friday: Pillar 3 (Building in public)

This ensures every pillar is covered, but Pillar 1 (agentic engineering) is most frequent because it's your primary positioning.

---

## The "never break the chain" rule

Once you start, **do not skip Monday-Wednesday-Friday posting for 90 days.** Even if a video is bad. Even if you're tired. Even if you're busy.

Why: algorithms reward consistency far more than quality. Three mediocre videos per week beats one perfect video per month. Always.

If you skip a day, restart the chain. Track it visibly. Make it a habit, not a decision.

---

## When to deviate from the plan

You should adjust when:

1. **A video goes unexpectedly viral** → make 3 more videos in that format immediately
2. **A topic gets tons of questions** → answer them in follow-up videos
3. **You ship a big Wokku feature** → that becomes the week's building-in-public content
4. **You hit a major milestone** (first 100 users, 1000 followers) → celebrate it publicly

Don't deviate when:

1. You're tired (tired posts better than no posts)
2. A video flops (keep going, next one might hit)
3. You're bored of the topic (your audience isn't bored)
4. You see someone else doing better (stay in your lane)
